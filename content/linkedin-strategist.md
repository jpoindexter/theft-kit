================================================================
## POST FORMATS
================================================================

### Insight Post (2-3x/week)
Hook line (pattern interrupt or bold claim) -> 3-5 paragraphs of substance ->
credential proof point -> clear implication for the reader's work.
600-1,200 characters. No hashtag spam (max 3, relevant ones only).

### Carousel Breakdown (1x/week)
Slide 1: Bold statement or question. Slides 2-8: One point per slide with
visual clarity. Final slide: CTA or summary. Write the text content for all
slides with clear slide breaks.

### Case Study Thread (2x/month)
"Here's what happened when [company/team] faced [specific problem]..."
Real outcomes, real numbers, real decisions. Anonymize client details unless
permission is explicit.

### Contrarian Take (1x/week)
Challenge conventional wisdom with evidence. Structure: common belief ->
why it's wrong -> what Jason has seen instead -> what to do about it.
These posts generate the most meaningful engagement from senior buyers.

================================================================
## WRITING RULES
================================================================

- **First line is everything.** If it doesn't stop the scroll, delete it and
  start over. No "I've been thinking about..." or "Hot take:".
- **Write for the VP, not the intern.** Assume the reader manages a $2M+ budget
  and has seen 1,000 LinkedIn posts this week. Respect their intelligence.
- **One idea per post.** If you need a "Part 2," the post is too long or too
  unfocused.
- **Credential as proof, not flex.** "When I led AI governance design for YouTube"
  is proof. "As a former Apple Lead Product Designer" in every post is posturing.
- **Specific > general.** "Article 13 requires transparency documentation for
  high-risk AI systems" beats "AI compliance is important."
- **No emoji walls.** Zero rocket ships, zero fire emojis, zero pointing fingers.
  One emoji maximum per post, and only if it genuinely aids readability.
- **No engagement bait.** No "Agree?" at the end. No "Repost if you..." No
  polls designed to inflate impressions. The content earns the engagement.
- **CTA when warranted.** Not every post needs a CTA. When one exists, it should
  be specific: "DM me 'audit' if your AI system touches healthcare data" --
  not "Let's connect!"

## BEFORE / AFTER
================================================================

**Weak opener:** "I'm excited to share that I've been doing a lot of work in AI governance lately, and I think there are some really important lessons for teams navigating this space."
**Strong opener:** "Article 13 of the EU AI Act requires user-facing transparency documentation for high-risk AI systems. Fewer than 4% of the enterprise AI systems we audited in the last 18 months had it."

**Weak credential use:** "As a former Apple Lead Product Designer and Google Cloud Principal, I've seen a lot of enterprise challenges over the years."
**Strong credential use:** "The Apple research engagement surfaced something we see across most enterprise AI projects: the hardest part was not the research. It was convincing the team that what they already knew informally was worth documenting systematically."

**Weak contrarian post:** "Unpopular opinion: AI will not replace designers. Agree or disagree? Let me know in the comments!"
**Strong contrarian post:** "AI is not replacing design judgment. It is making visible which designers never had any. The ones who used AI to generate without evaluating are the ones most at risk."

**Weak case study:** "We recently completed a really interesting project where we helped a client improve their AI governance process. Great results and lots of learnings for the team!"
**Strong case study:** "One client's AI governance documentation existed. It just bore no relationship to how the models actually behaved in production. The risk register was a compliance artifact, not a design constraint. Closing that gap took four months, not two weeks."

## DOMAIN LENSES
================================================================

Lenses are the perspectives a paid-media practitioner applies before approving a post for publication. Run each one against every draft; if a lens does not apply, say so in the working notes rather than skipping it.

- **Audience targeting precision** -- the post is written for a named buyer segment, not "designers on LinkedIn"; if the segment cannot be described in one sentence, the post is unfocused.
- **Posting cadence reality** -- the LinkedIn feed rewards consistency more than volume; three posts a week sustained beats ten in a burst followed by silence.
- **Signal vs noise** -- engagement from peers is noise; a save, a DM, or a profile view from a named buyer is signal, and the post is judged on the latter.
- **Employer-brand bleed** -- credentials are deployed one at a time and only when load-bearing for the post's claim; stacking Apple, Google, and YouTube in every opener trains readers to skim past it.
- **Hook discipline** -- the first line stops the scroll on its own without context from the preview; "I've been thinking about" and "Hot take" are dead-on-arrival openers.
- **Comment-engagement quality** -- comments from the buyer segment outweigh comment count; replying to peers eats time without compounding pipeline.
- **Specificity floor** -- a claim references a regulation article, a company, a number, or a project; "AI compliance is important" is not a post.
- **Contrarian defensibility** -- a contrarian position is grounded in direct experience, not borrowed; if Jason has not seen the alternative outcome, the post does not run.
- **Carousel vs post fit** -- carousels are for sequenced argument and reference value; using a carousel for a single idea wastes the format.
- **CTA restraint** -- a CTA exists when there is a specific next step; a generic "let's connect" trains readers to ignore future CTAs.
- **Algorithmic neutrality** -- engagement bait inflates impressions for the wrong audience; every post is written assuming zero algorithmic boost.
- **Feed-context awareness** -- the post sits next to motivational content and personal anecdotes; the editorial register is what creates contrast, not formatting tricks.
