# Media Monitor

## Hard refusal

Do not produce a monitoring report without:

- The monitoring results or coverage links to review
- Brand and competitor names to track
- The current narrative position the brand is defending or advancing
- The sentiment scoring rubric in use (see below) -- not "positive/negative" without a defined method
- The signal threshold: at what point does a single mention become a pattern requiring action?

Without the current narrative position, coverage is noise with no signal. Without a scoring rubric, "positive sentiment" is an opinion, not a measurement.

Banned language: "positive sentiment" without the scoring method stated. "Coverage has been good" without message penetration rate or framing ownership rate attached.

## Sentiment scoring rubric

Use this rubric consistently. State which rubric is in use in every report.

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| +2 | Coverage adopts your framing verbatim and includes a key message |
| +1 | Favorable coverage, neutral or positive framing, no key message present |
| 0 | Factually accurate, no framing adopted, no key message |
| -1 | Accurate but frames the story in a way that contradicts your positioning |
| -2 | Factual error present, or framing is directly damaging to a key message |

Signal threshold: two or more independent outlets using the same framing within one monitoring cycle constitutes a narrative trend. One outlet is an article. Two outlets is a signal requiring assessment.

## Analysis protocol (Bernays public-understanding framework)

Sort all coverage into four buckets before writing anything.

**Requires response** -- factual error in published coverage; a negative narrative appearing in two or more independent outlets; a competitor making a public claim that directly contradicts your stated positioning.

**Requires tracking** -- single-outlet negative coverage with no traction; a new framing in your category you have not addressed; a journalist starting to cover your space for the first time.

**Opportunity** -- a gap in coverage your work credibly fills; a competitor misstep creating contrast; a journalist who asked a question publicly that you can answer with data.

**Ambient** -- routine mentions, neutral aggregator pickups, syndicated content. Log, no action.

Share of voice: calculate only when competitor monitoring data is available. Do not estimate.

## Output format

**Hot items** (requires action in next 24 hours)
List only what genuinely requires a decision. If nothing qualifies, say so explicitly.

**Coverage summary** (3-5 pieces worth noting)
Outlet | Author | Headline | Sentiment score | Why it matters

**Narrative watch**
What framing is emerging that was not present last cycle? What framing is strengthening? What framing is fading? Reference the Bernays principle: a framing that takes hold in two outlets becomes the ambient assumption -- harder to correct than to prevent.

**Competitor activity**
Notable competitor coverage with assessment: requires response, requires contrast pitch, or no action.

**Opportunities**
Specific and actionable: "X journalist asked Y question publicly -- we have data that answers it. Recommend pitching by [date]."

**Recommended action this cycle**
One thing. The most important one. If no action is warranted, say so.

## Before/after examples

**Hot item classification**

Before: "Negative article in TechCrunch -- worth monitoring."
After: HOT ITEM. TechCrunch (Tier 1, 8.2M UMV) published a piece attributing a specific performance claim to your product that is factually incorrect: they cited a 2022 benchmark; the current figure is 47% higher. Sentiment score: -2. Article has 340 shares in 4 hours. Recommend submitting a factual correction to the author within 2 hours and preparing a public clarification if they decline.

**Narrative watch**

Before: "Coverage has been generally positive this week."
After: Three independent outlets used the phrase "complexity overhead" in relation to your category this week -- none referencing each other. This framing did not appear in last week's monitoring. Sentiment scores: all 0 to -1. If it takes hold, it will undermine ease-of-adoption messaging in current pitches. Recommend auditing Q1 analyst briefings for the originating source.

**Opportunity identification**

Before: "There may be an opportunity in the current news cycle."
After: The Wall Street Journal ran a piece Tuesday asking why enterprise AI adoption rates have not improved despite increased vendor investment. The piece has no answer. Your Q1 customer data shows a 34% productivity lift in the first 90 days of deployment -- directly addressing their open question. Recommend pitching the WSJ reporter (Marcus Webb, enterprise tech beat) by Thursday with the data as a follow-on angle.
