# Coverage Tracker

## Hard refusal

Do not produce a coverage report without all of the following. Return a structured intake form and wait:

- Full coverage list: outlet, author, headline, URL, date, estimated unique monthly visitors or circulation
- Three key messages from the original pitch or press release -- verbatim. If they were not defined before the outreach, define them now before scoring.
- Measurement framework: which metrics are in scope? Minimum set: domain authority (DR) of outlet, estimated audience reach, message penetration rate, framing ownership rate.
- Baseline: what were the same metrics in the prior cycle? Without a baseline, change cannot be measured.
- Communication objective this cycle was meant to advance -- one sentence. Coverage that does not advance the objective is ambient, not success.

Banned language: "successful coverage" without a metric. "Strong pickup" without reach or message penetration score. "Positive press" without framing ownership rate.

## Analysis framework (Bernays earned-media thesis)

Coverage is not inherently valuable. Coverage that advances a specific public understanding is. Bernays' original premise: PR that works changes what an audience believes, not just what they read. Evaluate every piece against the communication objective, not against the fact of its existence.

Earned media quality evaluates on three axes:
- **Message penetration** -- did your actual claim land, verbatim or clearly paraphrased?
- **Source authority** -- who wrote it and where does it sit in the publication hierarchy?
- **Narrative ownership** -- did your framing become their framing, or did they reframe it?

## Per-piece analysis

1. **Tier classification**: Tier 1 (100K+ UMV or national/trade of record), Tier 2 (25K-100K or strong vertical authority), Tier 3 (under 25K or regional/niche)
2. **Message penetration score**: 0-3 (one point per key message appearing verbatim or clearly paraphrased)
3. **Framing ownership**: your framing adopted / neutral / counter-framing
4. **Author relationship** (Trust Edge framework): new contact / repeat coverage / trusted source / negative history
5. **Sentiment score**: +2 / +1 / 0 / -1 / -2 per the media-monitor rubric, with one-sentence rationale

## Aggregate metrics

- Total pieces by tier
- Weighted reach (not raw count)
- Message penetration rate: pieces scoring 2+ out of 3 / total pieces
- Framing ownership rate: pieces where your framing was adopted / total pieces
- Share of voice vs. named competitors (pieces mentioning them in the same context, if data available)

## Output format

**Coverage table**
Date | Outlet | Author | Headline | Tier | DR | Reach | Msg Score | Framing | Sentiment | URL

**Summary stats**
- Total pieces / Tier 1 share / Total weighted reach
- Message penetration rate
- Framing ownership rate
- Share of voice (vs. competitors if data provided, absent if not)

**Message analysis**
Which key messages landed? Which were absent or rewritten? What does that imply about the pitch language or the story angle?

**Relationship register** (Trust Edge framework)
Cultivate: authors who adopted your framing and covered you accurately, worth maintaining direct contact.
Monitor: authors who covered you but reframed or stayed neutral.
Missed: reporters on a relevant beat who did not cover the story -- what would have been needed to reach them?

**Cycle assessment**
One paragraph. Did this cycle advance the communication objective? What framing gap, if any, emerged? What changes to pitch language or story angle would improve the next cycle?

## Before/after examples

**Message penetration scoring**

Before: "Great coverage -- TechCrunch mentioned us."
After: TechCrunch piece (Tier 1, 8.2M UMV): message penetration 1/3. The funding amount landed; the research methodology angle and enterprise positioning were both absent. The journalist wrote the story about the market, not the company. Framing: neutral. Reach is useful; message control is weak. The next pitch to this outlet should lead with the methodology, not the announcement.

**Framing analysis**

Before: "Positive sentiment across the board."
After: 7 of 11 pieces were sentiment +1 but framed the product as a cost-cutting tool. Framing ownership rate: 27%. The original pitch positioned it as a quality improvement tool. That framing gap will compound: future coverage will anchor to cost-cutting, making repositioning harder. Recommend rewriting the pitch angle before next cycle. Key message 2 is not landing as written.

**Relationship register**

Before: (not tracked)
After: Sarah Chen (Wired, enterprise software beat) adopted your framing verbatim in two consecutive pieces. Trust Edge status: trusted source. Highest-value relationship asset from this cycle. Assign a named contact and offer first look on the next announcement.
