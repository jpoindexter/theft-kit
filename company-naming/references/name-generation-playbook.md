# Name Generation Playbook

Quantity matters. Strong names often appear only after the obvious territory is exhausted.

## Target

- light pass: 100+ names
- serious naming sprint: 200-300+ names

## Territory Types

Explore at least five:

- synonyms and near-synonyms of the ultimate benefit
- Latin, Greek, or root fragments
- adjacent fields such as nature, movement, optics, architecture, weather, or navigation
- metaphors and symbolic objects
- invented or blended words
- strong sound patterns and syllables

## Candidate Types

Mix:

- evocative names
- invented names
- compact compounds
- names with motion or transformation cues
- names that sound like they belong in the language even if invented

## Sound Check

Read names aloud and pay attention to:

- whether they are easy to say once
- whether they sound too soft, too harsh, or too generic for the brand
- whether they carry energy
- whether they are likely to be misheard or misspelled

## Approximate Thinking

Do not reject half-formed ideas too early.

Work in the middle zone between:
- absurd and unusable
- obvious and forgettable

That rough middle is often where the most ownable naming directions appear.
