# Content Engine — Short-Form UGC Playbook

Reference for turning "what's working right now" into creator-style short-form
content without importing the shady parts of affiliate-marketing playbooks.

---

## What To Keep vs. What To Drop

Useful mechanics to keep:
- reverse-engineering proven clip structure instead of guessing
- building a hook library from real winners
- decomposing pacing, shot order, and proof placement
- making the CTA feel like a natural next step instead of a hard pivot
- creating several angle variants before rendering

Things to reject outright:
- geo spoofing or account-location manipulation
- account farming, reinstall loops, or shadowban "warming" rituals
- sweepstakes, gift-card, or lead-gen funnels disguised as product content
- fake reactions, fake testimonials, or implied brand partnerships
- brand-confusing domains or hidden commercial intent

If a reference article mixes both, say so plainly and continue with the
creative mechanics only.

## The UGC Reverse-Engineering Workflow

### 1. Collect winning examples

Start with `get_trending_content` for the topic and target platform. For
creator-style shorts, prefer examples that match the intended energy:
- talking-head discovery
- reaction or surprise
- before/after transformation
- "I found a faster way" demo
- myth-bust or contrarian explanation

Do not overfit to only giant accounts. Outliers on mid-sized accounts often
show fresher patterns.

### 2. Log the right fields

For each strong example, capture:
- hook type
- first on-screen visual
- POV ("I", "you", "we", observer)
- emotional cue (surprised, relieved, skeptical, obsessed)
- proof beat (demo, screenshot, metric, comparison, testimonial)
- reveal timing
- CTA type
- total duration and pacing notes

The goal is not "copy this clip." The goal is "understand what repeated shape
keeps earning attention."

### 3. Build pattern families

After 5-10 examples, group them into 3-5 repeatable families.

Common families:
- discovery: "I found a faster way to do X"
- reaction: "Wait, this actually fixed X?"
- proof-first: metric or result appears before explanation
- myth-bust: "Everyone says X. That is wrong."
- workflow reveal: problem -> shortcut -> payoff

### 4. Turn patterns into a hook library

Create 10-20 hooks across several angles, not tiny copy tweaks.

Good angle buckets:
- curiosity
- outcome
- pain point
- contrarian
- identity
- proof or social proof

Example shapes:
- "I was doing [task] the slow way until I tried this."
- "If you still do [task] manually, watch this."
- "This cut [painful task] from [old state] to [new state]."
- "The workflow everyone recommends for [job] is backwards."
- "I thought this was hype until it saved me [specific outcome]."

### 5. Write a beat sheet before the script

For short-form UGC, a beat sheet is usually more important than polished prose.

Recommended 15-second shape:
- `0:00-0:02` Hook or pattern interrupt
- `0:02-0:05` Context or problem
- `0:05-0:10` Proof or demo beat
- `0:10-0:13` Reveal or payoff
- `0:13-0:15` CTA

If the idea needs more than 15 seconds, do not force it through the default
agent-media path. Either tighten it to 15 seconds or switch to Seedance /
HyperFrames for a longer, more structured format.

The opener should show or imply the payoff fast. Do not hide the good part in
the back half.

### 6. Route to the right production path

- `agent-media-create-ugc-video` for creator-style talking-head, discovery, or
  first-person explanation
- `openrouter-video-create` when the idea is cinematic or scene-heavy instead of
  face-led
- `create_hyperframes_composition` when the content depends on explicit text,
  product UI, or deterministic graphic beats

## Creative Templates

### Discovery UGC

Best for:
- product discovery
- "I found a faster way"
- everyday workflow pain

Beat template:
- hook: surprising shortcut or time-save
- context: what used to be annoying
- proof: one concrete moment, screen, or stat
- payoff: easier day, faster output, less stress
- CTA: try it, watch demo, grab template, see how it works

### Reaction UGC

Best for:
- surprising product behavior
- feature reveals
- strong before/after moments

Beat template:
- hook: surprise first
- context: what triggered the reaction
- proof: show the result, not just the face
- payoff: why that matters
- CTA: one next step only

Do not use fake reaction bait. If the reaction is the concept, tie it to a real
feature or proof beat within the first few seconds.

### Founder or Operator Demo

Best for:
- B2B tools
- workflow automation
- time-saving products

Beat template:
- hook: "here's the faster way"
- context: painful manual process
- proof: 1-2 step walkthrough
- payoff: time, money, or sanity saved
- CTA: focused and practical

This template is often safer and stronger than trying to mimic consumer
affiliate-style clips for a serious product.

## CTA Rules

The CTA should match the energy of the clip.

Prefer:
- "See the workflow"
- "Try the template"
- "Watch the full demo"
- "Steal this setup"
- "Grab the checklist"

Avoid:
- abrupt hard sells that ignore the clip's promise
- bait-and-switch CTA language
- generic "link in bio" with no payoff framing

Lower-friction CTAs usually outperform heavy asks in organic UGC.

---

## Product-Safe Adaptation Rules

When the source material comes from affiliate marketers or arbitrage creators:
- keep the pattern extraction
- keep the hook-library idea
- keep the proof-before-explanation pacing
- discard the compliance hacks and offer-wall mindset

Reframe for product-led content:
- real product proof over fake scarcity
- review-gated drafts over spammy volume
- one honest claim over stacked hype
- repeatable format systems over throwaway gimmicks

That gives the team the durable upside of the reference without importing the
part that would damage trust.
